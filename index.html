About Me

Geospatial Intelligence for Sustainable development

I work on large-scale geospatial systems integrating remote sensing, machine learning and cloud computing for forest monitoring.

I am a Remote Sensing and GIS professional with over 7 years of experience in geospatial analysis, satellite data processing, forest fire monitoring, biomass carbon estimation, and Web GIS development. Currently working at the Forest Survey of India (FSI), Dehradun, I have contributed to several national-level projects involving Above Ground Biomass Estimation, Burnt Area Mapping, Forest Fire Alert Systems, and machine learning-based geospatial workflows using Python and Google Earth Engine. My expertise includes Remote Sensing, GIS, SAR and optical satellite data analysis, automation using Python, ArcPy, Google Earth Engine, and Web GIS technologies such as GeoServer, PostgreSQL, OpenLayers, and Leaflet. I have also worked extensively on forest fire assessment, carbon emission modelling, land use/land cover analysis, and environmental monitoring using multi-source geospatial data. Passionate about technology and innovation, I enjoy building automated geospatial solutions that support environmental conservation, disaster management, and sustainable resource planning.
